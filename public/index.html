Balance It Out: Algebra Challenge

How to Play

This interactive challenge helps you practise solving equations step by step. An equation is displayed with the left-hand side (LHS) and right-hand side (RHS) in separate boxes. Your aim is to isolate the variable x by performing the same operation on both sides.

The game has three types of equations:

  • Questions 1–10: Example: 3x + 5 = 11
  • Questions 11–20: Example: (2x + 4) ÷ 3 = 6
  • Questions 21–30: Example: 2x + 3 = 5x + 5

A balance scale visual shows how the equation compares to its original form. Click New Equation for a new problem (after 30 questions the game resets).

Operation Tips

When solving equations, you must perform the same operation on both sides to keep the equation balanced – just like a scale. Below are detailed tips for each operation with clear examples.

  • Addition:
    Tip: Adding a constant moves the entire expression up by that amount. When adding an x term (for Questions 21–30), you increase the coefficient of x.
    Example: If you have 3x + 5 = 11 and you add 2 to both sides, the equation becomes 3x + 7 = 13.
  • Subtraction:
    Tip: Subtracting a constant lowers the value of the expression. Subtracting an x term reduces the coefficient of x.
    Example: With 4x + 8 = 20, subtracting 8 from both sides gives 4x = 12.
  • Multiplication:
    Tip: Multiplying scales the entire expression. This is useful when you need to eliminate fractions or adjust coefficients.
    Example: For the equation (x + 3) ÷ 2 = 5, multiplying both sides by 2 gives x + 3 = 10.
  • Division:
    Tip: Dividing reduces the entire expression by a factor. This is often the final step to isolate x.
    Example: In 6x = 18, dividing both sides by 6 gives x = 3.

For equations with variables on both sides (e.g. 2x + 3 = 5x + 5), your goal is to collect all the x terms on one side and the constants on the other. Remember: every operation performed on one side must also be performed on the other.
